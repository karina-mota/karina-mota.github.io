Sprint 1- Cultural Blog

Using the evidence gathered, describe your core personal values.
Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values
involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.

In a former job I made a mistake that I was lucky to discover in time to fix and ended up
not having any effect on the client. However, I decided to share my near miss with my manager, which
made the company review and change their process which brought more security to their employees and clients.

Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

I grew up in a not so wealthy family during an economical crisis in Brazil.
This led my parents to work very hard to be able to provide the basic needs of the family.
My environment was based on hard work and creativity to make ends meet.

My parents decision to immigrate to another country to find work has been an shift in our lives.
We learned to adapt to many circumstances and to work even harder.
Although financial hardship was something present in our lives, my parents have always
managed to help others either by providing accommodation, help with work, food or income.
Today I have more empathy towards others and am always willing to help those in need.

Using the evidence gathered, describe your strengths and limitations.
Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development.

I'm a hard worker and very adaptable to different work environments. I'm open to new ideas and try
have a growth mindset on a daily basis.
As per my limitation to open myself to others can be challenging to my colleagues as I will have
difficulty expressing my true feelings.

Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey
and how they might intersect with learning obstacles.

I like to dig into the root of a problem to find a solution, which I believe will benefit my learning strategies.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others,
but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective
they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

A colleague and I were trying to implement a new process in our workplace. The manager had assigned me
to run the new process and gave me the instructions. My colleague wasn't accepting the instructions given
and wanted to do it another way. After having little progress with the project, our manager had a meeting
with both of us and gave my colleague the same instructions I had been giving throughout the project.
After the meeting my colleague had no resistance and followed with the instructions correctly.

My colleauge's attitude made me feel insignificant at the time. However, today I realise I could've not been
clear with the instructions and should've asked my colleague for feedback.
