What is Emotional Intelligence?

EQ is defined as the individual ability to identify, evaluate, control and express emotions.
We don’t have much control over the emotions we experience at a given moment but we can control
our reaction to those emotions by focusing on our thoughts.

Five components of EQ:
Self-awareness - the ability to recognize and understand one’s mood, motivations
and abilities. Also understand the effect they have on others.

Self-regulation - the ability to control one’s impulses, the ability to think before
you speak/react.

Motivation - is having the interest in learning and self-improvement.
It is the strength to keep going when there are obstacles. Is setting a goal and following through with them.

Empathy - it is the ability to understand other people’s emotion and reaction.
Empathy can only be achieved if self-awareness is achieved. One must be able to understand themselves
before they can understand others.

Social Skills - is the ability to maintain friendship and relationships and finding common
ground with them.


How is it different to IQ?


IQ is the ability to learn, understand and apply information to skills. It is designed to assess an
individual intelligence level, which EQ is the ability to understand a person’s emotion.


Why is it important?


People with high EQ usually makes great leaders and team players because of their ability to
understand, empathize and connect with people around them. While people with high IQ just means
that a person can have ability to technical skills but doesn’t mean that they will be able to work
well as a team player or be a good team leader.
