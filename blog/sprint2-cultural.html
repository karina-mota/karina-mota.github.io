Strength and Limitations

If not well balanced, my strength and limitations have both positive and
negative aspects.

Strength:

    No matter the curve ball life has thrown, I’ve always kept my pace and moved
    forward. This has made me resilient and adaptive. I’m not afraid to take a new path
    in search of a better outcome.     I don’t get put off when I have a problem. I usually like to dig deep into a problem,
    understand what is causing it and find a solution. I like to identify the root cause to a
    problem to prevent the same error in the future.

Limitation:

    My strength to dig deep into a problem can also be my limitation.
    As I am too meticulous, I can dig myself into a rabbit hole and then have to rush to meet a deadline.
     My decision making skills are far from being the best.
    I tend to weight both sides of a decision which makes me extremely
    indecisive and fear of missing out of the unchosen path.
    Opening myself to others is a very hard process for me.

Planning and commitment & Managing Workload

My biggest challenge is to organise my hours during weekdays to study. I work full time and I arrive
home late from work and it can be quite overwhelming to jump to a computer after spending more than 8 hours
in front of a computer at work. My weeknight routine includes household chores, organize/get ready for the night and following day,
meal prepping and studying. In order to be able to have study time after work, I’ve established to wake up between 5-5:30am on
m ost weekdays to make sure I get enough time to do my morning stretch exercises and meditation before work.
